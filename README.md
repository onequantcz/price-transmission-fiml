Agricultural Price Transmission Analysis

Motivation

Price changes in agricultural markets rarely affect all participants equally. A shock originating at the farm level may be amplified, delayed, or partially absorbed before reaching processors and final consumers.

Traditional approaches often model price transmission as a one-directional process, assuming that prices move sequentially from producers to processors and finally to consumers. However, real markets are characterized by feedback effects, market power, information delays, and strategic behavior among participants.

Understanding these relationships is important not only for forecasting prices, but also for evaluating market efficiency, identifying transmission bottlenecks, and assessing how economic shocks propagate through the supply chain.

This project investigates the transmission of flour prices across the Czech agricultural supply chain, focusing on the relationships between agricultural producer prices, industrial producer prices, and consumer prices.
